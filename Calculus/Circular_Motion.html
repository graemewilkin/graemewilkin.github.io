Velocity and acceleration for circular motion

Back to Calculus homepage


The following picture shows a point moving around a circle of radius \(1\) with parametric equations \( (x(t), y(t)) = (\cos(t), \sin(t)) \). The velocity vector is then \( (x'(t), y'(t)) = (-\sin(t), \cos(t)) \) and the acceleration vector is \( (x''(t), y''(t)) = (-\cos(t), -\sin(t)) \).

You can see the velocity and acceleration on the picture below. At each point the velocity is tangent to the curve (this is true for any parametric curve). A feature of circular motion is that the acceleration vector always points towards the centre of the circle (you can see this from the equations \( (x''(t), y''(t)) = (-\cos(t), -\sin(t)) = -(x(t), y(t)) \)).
