The following picture shows the graph of \(y=x^2\). The blue line connecting the points \(A\) and \(B\) approximates the tangent line of the graph (which is red). By moving the points \(A\) and \(B\), you can see how the slope of the difference quotient gets closer to the slope of the tangent line as \(B\) approaches \(A\). You can also zoom in to the picture to get a better view.
