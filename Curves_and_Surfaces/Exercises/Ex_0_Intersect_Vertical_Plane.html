Intersecting a vertical plane with hyperboloids and spheres

Back to Curves and Surfaces homepage

The following pictures show the intersection of the plane \(ax + by = 0\) with different surfaces in \(\mathbb{R}^3\). The first picture shows the intersection of this plane with the unit sphere \[\{ (x, y, z) \in \mathbb{R}^3 \, : \, x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1 \}. \] The animation shows the plane and the curve of intersection for different values of \(a\) and \(b\). As you can see, this intersection is always a circle, which matches the algebraic proof in the problem solutions.

The next picture is of the intersection of the plane \(ax + by = 0\) with the hyperboloid of one sheet \[ \{ (x, y, z) \in \mathbb{R}^3 \, : \, x^2 + y^2 - z^2 = 1 \} \] Again, the animation shows the plane and the curve of intersection for different values of \(a\) and \(b\). This time the intersection is always a hyperbola, which matches the algebraic proof in the problem solutions.


Finally, we have the same picture for the hyperboloid of two sheets. Again, the intersection is a hyperbola, although it is oriented differently to the previous example.
