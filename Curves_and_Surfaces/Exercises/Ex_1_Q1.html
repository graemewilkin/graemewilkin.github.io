Visualising the curve from Q1 on Exercise Sheet 1

The following picture shows the curve \[ p : \mathbb{R} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^3, \quad p(s) = \left(3 \, arcsinh \left( \frac{s}{5} \right), \sqrt{25+s^2}, 4 \, arcsinh \left( \frac{s}{5} \right) \right) \] used in Q1 from Exercise Sheet 1. You can click and drag on the point \(P\) to move the point along the curve and see how the Frenet frame changes. You can click and drag to rotate the picture and change the viewpoint. By choosing the right viewpoint, it is easy to see that the curve lies in a plane. We can also prove this from the fact that the binormal vector is constant (you will prove this as part of the exercise), and therefore the torsion is zero, which implies that the curve is planar.

Note. In order to make the Frenet frame visible in the above picture, I have scaled the vectors \(T, N, B\) by a fixed amount. They are no longer unit vectors, however they do point in the same direction in order to give you an idea of how the Frenet frame changes.
