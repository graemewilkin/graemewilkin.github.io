The Gauss map for the hyperbolic paraboloid

Back to Curves and Surfaces homepage


The following picture shows the hyperbolic paraboloid as the graph of the function \(f(u,v)=0.3 u^2 - 0.3 v^2\). The point \(p(u,v) = (u,v,f(u,v))\) is plotted on the surface, and by moving the sliders on the left of the picture you can move this point around the surface by changing the values of \(u\) and \(v\).

The normal vector \(N\) is drawn pointing upwards from the surface. The Gauss map is represented by the vector on the unit sphere centred at \((0,0,4)\) in the picture.

By moving the point around the surface, you can see how the Gauss map changes. In particular, you can see the negative Gauss curvature of the surface at \((0,0,0)\) by observing the change in the Gauss map as you vary the values of \(u\) and \(v\).
