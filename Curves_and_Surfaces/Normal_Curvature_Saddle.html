Normal curvatures of a saddle point

Back to Curves and Surfaces homepage


The following picture shows the hyperbolic paraboloid as the graph of the function \(f(u,v)=0.3 u^2 - 0.3 v^2\), together with a plane through the origin which contains the normal vector to the hyperbolic paraboloid. The curve of intersection between this plane and the surface is also drawn in the diagram.

By clicking and dragging the slider, you can change the angle \(\theta\), which will rotate the plane around the \(z\)-axis of the diagram. As you do this, you can see how the curve of intersection between the plane and the surface changes.

You can now see that the surface has negative Gauss curvature, since for \(\theta = 0^\circ\) or \(\theta = 180^\circ\) the curve of intersection curves away from the upwards normal vector of the surface, and for \(\theta = 90^\circ\) or \(\theta = 270^\circ\) the curve of intersection curves towards the upwards normal vector.
