A parametrised torus with a normal plane

Back to Curves and Surfaces homepage


The following picture shows parametrised torus in \(\mathbb{R}^3\), together with a plane through the origin which intersects the torus in a circle.

By clicking and dragging the point \(G\) (initially on the \(x\)-axis), you can move the plane around the torus.

The intersection of the plane and the torus is a circle of radius \(b\). You can see visually that one of the principal curvatures will always be \(\kappa_1 = \pm\frac{1}{b}\) (where the sign depends on the orientation of the normal vector).

By comparing the different points on this circle, you can see the elliptic (\(\kappa_2\) has the same sign as \(\kappa_1\)), parabolic (\(\kappa_2 = 0\) and \(\kappa_1 \neq 0\)) and hyperbolic (\(\kappa_2\) has the opposite sign to \(\kappa_1\)) points on the torus.
