The following is a picture of the hyperbolic paraboloid from Question 3 on worksheet 2 (the case \(r=-1\)). The variable \(h\) denotes the height of the horizontal plane, and you can adjust this by moving the slider in the diagram. The intersection of the horizontal plane with the hyperbolic paraboloid gives us the level set of the surface, and by moving this plane you can see how the level sets change for the three cases: \(h > 0\), \(h = 0\) and \(h < 0\).

Remember that you can also click and drag the figure to rotate your viewpoint.
