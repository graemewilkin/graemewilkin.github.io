Projecting a cube onto a plane

Back to Multiple View Geometry homepage


In the following picture, a cube is projected onto the blue plane using the point \(C\) as a point of perspective. This models the pinhole camera studied in lectures.

You can click and drag the point \(C\) to move it around the picture. Notice how the projection of the cube changes. By taking different view points (corresponding to different choices of the point \(C\)) we can see the "three dimensionality" of the cube on the 2D image plane.
