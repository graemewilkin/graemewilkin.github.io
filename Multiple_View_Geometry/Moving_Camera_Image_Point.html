What happens to an image when the camera moves along a line?

Back to Multiple View Geometry homepage


The following picture shows a point \(P\) projected onto two different image planes. The black line is the camera baseline that joins the two camera centres \(C_1 C_2\), and the two epipoles \(E_1\) and \(E_2\) are the intersections of this line with the image planes.

You can click and drag the point \(E_2\) to move the image plane along the camera baseline \(C_1 C_2\), and the camera centre \(C_2\) will move as well.

Notice that the point \(Q_2\) always moves along the blue line, which is the intersection of the second image plane with the red plane (defined as the plane passing through \(P\) and the line \(C_1 C_2\)). Note that this blue line also passes through the epipolar point \(E_2\).

As you move the image plane by dragging the epipolar point \(E_2\) far away from \(P\), the point \(Q_2\) will move closer to \(E_2\).

For the case of a single point, this picture illustrates that when the camera centre moves along a line, the projection of the point on the image plane moves along a line radiating out from the epipolar point.
