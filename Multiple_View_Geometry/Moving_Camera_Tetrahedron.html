What happens to an image when the camera moves along a line?

Back to Multiple View Geometry homepage


Along with this example, here is another example of a camera moving along a line. In the following picture, the camera centre \(C_1\) is fixed, and the camera centre \(C_2\) moves along the camera baseline \(C_1 M\). The two epipoles \(E_1\) and \(E_2\) are defined as the intersection of the camera baseline with the two image planes.

You can click and drag the point \(E_2\) to move it along the camera baseline. As you do this, you can see how the projection of the tetrahedron moves on the image plane.

In fact, the image of the tetrahedron always moves along lines radiating out from the epipole \(E_2\). This is explained in more detail in this example.
