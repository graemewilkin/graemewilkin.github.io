Description of Research

My research centres around using Morse theory to study the topology of quotients of singular spaces, in particular those that arise from gauge-theoretical constructions (such as moduli spaces of Higgs bundles and Nakajima quiver varieties). The equations defining these spaces originate in physics as special cases of the Yang-Mills equations, and there exist deep connections between these moduli spaces and many other areas of mathematics, such as low-dimensional topology, geometric representation theory and the geometric Langlands program. In addition, these equations and their moduli spaces of solutions also have applications to control theory and computer network reliability.

Current Projects

  • Investigating links between these Morse-theoretic methods and Nakajima's work in Geometric Representation theory.
  • Using Morse theory on singular spaces to compute the cohomology of quiver varieties.
  • Constructing ancient solutions of curvature flows such as the Yang-Mills and Yang-Mills-Higgs flow.
