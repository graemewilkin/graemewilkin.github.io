Abstracts

16 August. Colin Tan (NUS)
Title. Archimedean Positivstellensätze
Abstract. Several archimedean Positivstellensätze for polynomials, including Handelman's Positivstellensatz and Pólya's Positivstellensatz, are known to be consequences of the Representation Theorem for a module over an archimedean semiring. Using verbatim Burgdorf, Scheiderer and Schweighofer's arguments using pure states, the Representation Theorem generalizes to a version for a semimodule over an archimedean semiring. We apply this generalized Representation Theorem to deduce generalizations of the Handelman's Positivstellensatz and Pólya's Positivstellensatz to matrix polynomials, due to Lê-Du' and Scherer-Hol respectively.

6 September. Dmitri Zaitsev (Trinity College, Dublin)
Title. CR geometry meets subelliptic PDEs
Abstract. In his seminal work from 1979, Joseph J. Kohn invented his theory of multiplier ideal sheaves connecting a priori estimates for the d-bar problem with local boundary invariants constructed in purely algebraic way.
I will explain the origin and motivation of the problem, and how Kohn's algorithm reduces it to a problem in local CR geometry of the boundary of a domain.
I will then present my recent work with Sung Yeon Kim based on the technique of jet vanishing orders, and show how it can be used to control the effectivity of multipliers in Kohn's algorithm, subsequently leading to precise a priori estimates.

11 October. Wu Chengyuan (NUS)
Title. Weighted Persistent Homology
Abstract. In this talk we introduce the theory of weighted persistent homology. We first generalize the definitions of weighted simplicial complex and the homology of weighted simplicial complex to allow weights in an integral domain R. Then we study the resulting weighted persistent homology. We show that weighted persistent homology can tell apart filtrations that ordinary persistent homology does not distinguish. For example, if there is a point considered as special, weighted persistent homology can tell when a cycle containing the point is formed or has disappeared.

25 October. Fedor Pavutnitskiy (NUS)
Title. Quillen homology and cohomology of a morphism
Abstract. In this talk we will discuss how familiar notion of (co)homology of a map can be described as a derived functor in sense of Quillen. Such point of view allows us to define the universal cocycles in cohomology of a morphism and the corresponding universal principal bundles. The lower central series of a group will be our main example here, and, if time permits, connections with Postnikov and Whitehead towers, as well as with Adams spectral sequence will be discussed.

22 November. Duc Viet Vu (KIAS)
Title. Monge-Ampere measures with Holder continuous potentials
Abstract. Let \(X\) be a compact Kahler manifold with Kahler form \(\omega\). A Monge-Ampere measure with Holder continuous potential on \(X\) is the self-intersection of maximal degree of a closed positive \((1,1)\)-current of Holder continuous potential which is cohomologous to \(\omega\). Denote by \(A\) the class of such measures. By fundamental works of Yau and Kolodziej on the complex Monge-Ampere equations, every measure with \(L^p\) densities (\(p>1\)) belongs to \(A\). In this talk, we will give an overview on recent results on the class \(A\).

23 November. Teo Lee Peng (Xiamen University, Malaysia)
Title. Moduli Space of Riemann Surfaces and Universal Teichmüller Space
Abstract. The moduli space of a surface is the space of all different complex structures on the surface. The moduli space of a hyperbolic surface is a complex manifold equipped with a Kahler metric - the Weil-Petersson metric, which is induced by the natural inner product of quadratic differentials. In this talk, we discuss the Weil-Petersson geometry of the moduli spaces of hyperbolic surfaces with finite area, as well as the universal Teichmüller space (the universal moduli space). In particular, we discuss the construction of the potential of the Weil-Petersson metric - the classical Liouville action which was motivated by string theory. We demonstrate the relation between the Liouville action and the renormalized volume of a corresponding hyperbolic three manifold. We also discuss the local index theorem on the moduli spaces.
